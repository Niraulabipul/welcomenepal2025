Traditional Nepalese Snacks

Relish the diverse flavors of Nepalese appetizers and street food

Sel Roti and Chiya

Sel Roti is a traditional rice flour doughnut, crispy on the outside and soft inside. It’s a must-have snack during festivals and special occasions in Nepal.

Jhol Momos

The beloved Nepali dumplings filled with meat or vegetables, served with soup, are the perfect snack for any time of the day.

Chatpate

A popular street food, Chatpate is a tangy and spicy mix of puffed rice, lentils, chopped onions, chilies, and achar. It’s a burst of flavors in every bite!

Explore More

Pakoda

Deep-fried fritters made from vegetables like onions, potatoes, or spinach, mixed in a spiced gram flour batter. Pakoda is a crispy tea-time favorite.

Explore More

Sukuti

Sukuti, or dried meat, is a traditional Nepali snack that is roasted and seasoned for a savory, chewy treat often enjoyed with local drinks.

Explore More

Chiura with Curry

Flattened rice (chiura) served with a side of spicy meat or vegetable curry is a popular snack, often enjoyed during casual gatherings or festivals.

The Culture of Nepalese Snacks

Snacks in Nepal are more than just food; they represent the country’s rich culinary diversity. From fried Sel Roti to spicy Chatpate, every snack carries a unique flavor and tradition cherished by locals and visitors alike.
