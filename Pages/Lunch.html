Traditional Nepalese Lunch

Discover the flavors and traditions of Nepalese mid-day meals

Dal Bhat Tarkari

A classic Nepalese lunch, Dal Bhat Tarkari includes lentil soup (dal), steamed rice (bhat), and a variety of vegetable curries (tarkari). Often complemented with achar (pickle), this is a staple meal.

Thakali Set

A traditional Thakali lunch features rice, dal, gundruk (fermented leafy greens), meat or vegetable curry, and pickles. Its balanced taste and variety make it a favorite for lunch.

Chatamari

Often called the "Nepalese Pizza," Chatamari is a rice flour crepe topped with minced meat, eggs, and vegetables. It's a delicious and light lunch option from the Newari community.

Aloo Tama

Aloo Tama is a tangy and spicy curry made with potatoes (aloo) and bamboo shoots (tama), sometimes with black-eyed peas. It's served with rice for a flavorful Nepalese lunch.

Sel Roti and Curry

Sel Roti, a traditional rice flour doughnut, is often paired with vegetable curry or yogurt for a light yet satisfying lunch. It's a popular meal during festivals and gatherings.

The Essence of Nepalese Lunch

Lunch in Nepal is a delightful journey of flavors, from the hearty Dal Bhat Tarkari to the exotic Chatamari and Aloo Tama. Every dish reflects the diversity and cultural heritage of Nepal, ensuring a wholesome dining experience.
